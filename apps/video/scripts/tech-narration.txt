Relay turns one confirmed customer brief into multiple verifiable offers. Here is how.

The Nuxt experience sends a Zod-validated brief to the NestJS API. PostgreSQL stores an immutable version, then Relay writes the negotiation run and its outbox event in one transaction.

Redis and BullMQ move identifiers—not transcripts or secrets—to a long-running worker. Jobs are idempotent, retry-safe, and calls run in parallel.

Provider adapters keep infrastructure replaceable. Google Places discovers businesses. ElevenLabs, through a native Twilio number, runs the calls. OpenAI converts transcripts into structured outcomes. Private recordings and evidence live in Supabase Storage.

Then deterministic domain policy takes over: normalize every fee, reject stale or unsupported leverage, rank only evidenced quotes, and attach every recommendation to its source.

Progress events stream back through the API, so customers see calls, quote movement, and final reasoning as it happens.

One immutable brief. Durable orchestration. Truthful negotiation. Verifiable decisions. That is Relay.
